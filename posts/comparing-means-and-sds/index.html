Comparing means and SDs

Many different questionnaires exists measuring the same constructs. For example, the NEO-PI and the BFI both measure the Big Five personality traits. Say, we want to compare reported means and standard deviations (SDs) for these questionnaires, and both use a Likert scale. This requires transforming the means and SDs to similar scales. The formulas for these transformations are derived below. We do this by first describing what numbers are reported by the studies.

In this post, I stick to the set theory convention of denoting sets by uppercase letters. Some studies report an averaged mean m‾\overline{m}m, that is,

m‾=m∣Q∣=∑T∣P∣⋅∣Q∣. \overline{m} = \frac{m}{|Q|} = \frac{\sum T}{|P| \cdot |Q|}. m=∣Q∣m​=∣P∣⋅∣Q∣∑T​.

To scale the standard deviation, we can use the equation for a linear transformation of the variance.

Let XXX be a random variable with finite mean μ\muμ and variance σ2\sigma^2σ2. Then for all constants aaa and bbb (Hogg et al. (2018)),

Var(aX+b)=a2⋅Var(X). Var(aX + b) = a^2 \cdot Var(X). Var(aX+b)=a2⋅Var(X).

From which follows that, for a random variable XXX and all constants aaa and bbb,

sd(aX+b)=Var(aX+b)=a2⋅Var(X)=∣a∣⋅sd(X). sd(aX + b) = \sqrt{Var(aX + b)} = \sqrt{a^2 \cdot Var(X)} = |a| \cdot sd(X). sd(aX+b)=Var(aX+b)​=a2⋅Var(X)​=∣a∣⋅sd(X).

So, for the averaged standard deviation s‾\overline{s}s,

s‾=sd(T)∣Q∣ \overline{s} = \frac{sd(T)}{|Q|} s=∣Q∣sd(T)​

Let klk_lkl​ and kuk_uku​ be respectively the lower and upper bound for the Likert scale per answer, that is, ∀r[r∈R:kl≤r≤ku]\forall_r [ r \in R : k_l \leq r \leq k_u]∀r​[r∈R:kl​≤r≤ku​]. Note that I have not taken Bessel's correction into account, which might be an issue for studies having small sample sizes. I will add that as soon as I understand how to take that correction into account.
